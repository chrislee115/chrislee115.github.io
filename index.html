About Me

    I was born in Troy, MI and went to Warren Consolidated Schools. Then right after 6th grade, we moved far - to the other side of Troy, MI, where I went to Troy School District. Somewhere along the way, I picked up a strong fascination with computer science (probably because I thought computer science = gaming), and I've carried that passion for the field ever since.

    In high school, I tried to push myself to learn cybersecurity through various CTFs and websites or learn programming through Codeacademy but I couldn't find the motivation to keep going when I couldn't figure something out - no one else around me had any knowledge in the field (I've become much better at Googling problems since then). Because of this ordeal, it became a really easy decision to enroll at the Univesity of Michigan to study Computer Science.

    My time here at UM has been wonderful thus far - Programming is now a way for me to create products that can provide actual benefit to people as opposed to a "hello world" hat trick, I am now understanding all different stacks of technology and how I can apply them to future projects instead of tunneling on trying to create the next Flappy Bird, and most importantly, I now know how to code websites as opposed to inspecting element on various pages and changing text to "butt."
